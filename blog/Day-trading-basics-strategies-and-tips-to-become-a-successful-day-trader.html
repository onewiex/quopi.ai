Day trading basics – strategies and tips to become a successful day trader

  • May 26, 2023
  • Trading Insights

Day trading is one of the most effective ways to make money online and work from wherever you wish. It involves buying and selling financial instruments within the same trading day to profit from short-term changes in price. However, day trading is not as easy as it may sound because it requires discipline, knowledge, and the use of the right strategies to benefit from it.


In this article, we will help you learn the basics of day trading and the key strategies that you must use to benefit from this endeavor. Whether you're new to trading or an experienced trader, we will provide you with valuable insights and practical strategies to navigate the dynamic market of day trading. Without wasting any more of your time, let's jump right in!


Tips to Become a successful day trader


Start Small

If you are new to day trading, it is always best to start with a small trading account and trade with a limited amount of capital. The definition of starting small may vary depending on one's income. For some, starting small may mean investing $50,000 in day trading, while for others, $5,000 may be what they can afford to invest in day trading.


The key is to determine an amount that you can comfortably allocate without feeling like you are risking too much. This approach allows you to gain experience, learn from the market, and minimize the likelihood of making significant losses. Starting small also helps manage emotions and reduces the pressure to engage in large, risky trades.


Set Aside Time

If you want to become a successful day trader, you need to give it enough time every day. Day trading requires focused attention and active participation in the market for several hours every day. It's essential to set aside dedicated time for trading, free from distractions, to closely monitor the markets, analyze charts, and execute trades.


By creating a routine and allocating specific hours for trading, you can maintain discipline and stay engaged in the dynamic nature of day trading. If you have a job or other engagements that won’t let you dedicate several hours every day to day trading, then it may not be for you.


Use Limit Orders to minimize losses.

A limit order requires the trader to set a specific price they’re willing to buy or sell a given stock. Traders determine this price depending on the history of the particular stock they want to trade. Placing a stop-loss order at a predetermined price level allows you to automatically exit a trade if the price moves against you.


This helps protect your trading capital and prevents substantial losses. By setting a limit order, you can ensure that your losses are controlled and that you exit trades when they no longer align with your initial analysis.


Avoid Penny Stocks

For those who may not know, penny stocks are low-priced stocks (usually under $5) with small market capitalization and limited liquidity. They are often associated with higher volatility and increased risk. These stocks can also be easily delisted from major stock exchange markets, which could lead to huge losses.


If you want to become a successful day trader, it is generally recommended to avoid penny stocks, as they can be manipulated, have wide bid-ask spreads, and lack reliable information. Unless you have done thorough research about the stock and you’re confident that it will work out, focus on trading more liquid and actively traded stocks.


Time Those Trades

As a day trader, one of the major factors that will determine your success is knowing when to buy and when to sell. Timing is crucial in day trading. You must identify optimal entry and exit points based on market trends, technical indicators, and price patterns. By carefully timing your trades, you can improve the likelihood of capturing favorable price movements and maximizing profits.


Timing trades will typically involve analyzing market conditions, identifying potential support and resistance levels, and utilizing tools like real-time charts and indicators. The middle hours of the day are usually less volatile and more ideal for beginners. Rush hours usually yield better opportunities as the market tends to become more volatile. However, rush hours are ideal for experienced traders who know the best decisions to make in such situations.


Be Realistic About Profits

- The main goal for getting into day trading is to get profits and generate some income. However, it is crucial to set realistic expectations regarding profits in day trading. While it is possible to make substantial gains, it is also important to acknowledge that day trading involves risks, and some trades may generate losses.


- So, you must set realistic profit goals to avoid the urge to overtrade or chase unrealistic gains when trading. Your goal should be generating consistent and steady profits over time since it is often more achievable and sustainable than seeking large gains in every trade.


Don’t make decisions based on your emotions.

As humans, we often make most of our decisions based on how we feel about something. However, making decisions based on emotions can sometimes lead us to the wrong path, especially with things like day trading. To become a successful day trader, it is important to remain calm, disciplined, and objective in your trading approach.


Don’t let fear or greed drive your decisions regarding when to enter or exit a trade. Develop a trading plan, stick to your strategies, and avoid impulsive decisions based on emotions. It is also crucial to avoid being intoxicated during trading.


Final thoughts


We have explored some of the best strategies and tips that you can utilize to optimize your day trading endeavors. By implementing these strategies, you can effectively capitalize on short-term price movements and maximize your profits. It is crucial for day traders to continuously learn, practice, and adapt their strategies to the ever-changing market conditions. The trading market is highly dynamic and requires dynamic strategies to navigate its complexities successfully.
